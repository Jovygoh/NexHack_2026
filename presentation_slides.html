Why checking contracts is hard for Malaysian SMEs

Problem Statement
RM 500+

Too Expensive

Hiring a lawyer to read a simple contract (like a room rental, NDA, or job offer) costs between RM500 to RM2,500 per document, which is too much for small businesses.

Law Updates

Too Many Law Changes

Laws like the Employment Act (about leave and working hours) and PDPA (privacy) change often. It is hard for normal business owners to keep up.

3 - 7 Days

Too Slow

Waiting for a lawyer to check a document takes days. This delays hiring staff or closing business deals when speed matters.

The ContractSense Solution

Solution

Automated Compliance Auditing

  • Double-Layer Audit: Combines deterministic rule scanning for instant vulnerability detection with heuristic LLM contextual checking.
  • Simple & Fast: SMEs can drop any agreement file, scan it, and see compliance flaws highlighted in under 30 seconds.
  • Secure Process: In-memory dynamic text extraction. Keep user files isolated without training public AI models on private enterprise contracts.

Core Product Features

Features
🔍

Quick Rule Scanner

Regex-based matcher parses contracts to instantly flag auto-renewals, hidden fees, unilateral changes, and liability limitations in under 1 second.

🧠

Smart AI Checker

Contextual LLM reviews terms against Malaysian regulations database (Employment Act 1955, PDPA 2010) and uploaded company compliance policy handbooks.

💡

Direct Fix Suggestions

Generates side-by-side highlighted redline corrections, complete with copy-pasteable safe compliance text to replace the bad parts.

🛡️ Data Privacy Assurance
We process everything dynamically in memory. The backend does not write contract binaries in public storage. No uploaded contract is sent to train public LLM foundational models, protecting private business information.

Improving Email Scanner Privacy (Technical Roadmap Item)

Right now, the scanner filters emails using keywords. In the future, we will connect securely using **Gmail/Outlook Sign-in (OAuth)**. The scanner will only check emails sent to a specific address (e.g. `contracts@company.com`) or labeled as `contracts`, keeping your personal emails completely private.
